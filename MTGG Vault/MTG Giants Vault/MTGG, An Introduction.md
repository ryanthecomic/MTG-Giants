# Overview

Magic The Gathering: Giants or MTGG is a fanmade project to integrate 4-player multiplayer in a game like Magic Arena.

The game supports a 2v2-commandesque format inspired by the Two Headed Giant format, in which i have named [The Giants Format] for simpler reference in form of acronym (MTGG)

MTGG will present user friendly UI and a Rules Engine provided by the Open Source code of XMage/Forge.
# The Giants Format

- Your Team shares a life total and tries to defeat a Team of two players
- Each player has their own: Deck, Mana, Spells and Commander
- Teams share their Phases and Life Totals: One upkeep, one attack phase, and one end step for each team.
-
- Each team starts with 30 life.
- A team loses if it has 15 or more poison counters.
- Best of one.
- Each player gets one free mulligan.
- The team that goes first does not get to draw a card.
-
- You cannot use your mana to cast spells for your teammate.
- You cannot equip your teammate's creature with your equipments.
- "You" still refers to you alone. Not both you and your teammate.
- You can look at your teammate's cards in hand or hidden zones.
- You can discuss strategies with your teammate.
- You can't pass notes or manipulate your teammate's cards.
- You and your teammate attack and block together.
	- If a card needs or trigger abilities with other creatures to attacking with it, your teammate's creatures can count (unless it says specifically: "Creatures you control" ).
- Any attack or block must be valid as a team to be allowed.
	- Both players on the team are considered the Attacking Player or the Defending Player.,

# Key Phrases

"You"/"You control": This means YOU and NOT your teammate

"Each player": There are two players in each team so this would trigger one time for each player it applies to.

"Each opponent": There are two opponents on the opposing team, so this would trigger two times.

# End Game

- When you get to 0 life.
- When you have 15 or more poison counters.
- If a player draws a card from an empty library.
- If anyone in a team wins or loses, the whole team wins or loses.
